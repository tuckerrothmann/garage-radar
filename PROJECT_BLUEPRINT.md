# Garage Radar

## One-line concept
A market intelligence and comps tracker for vintage/classic vehicles that aggregates listings, recent sales, and vehicle-specific details to spot pricing signals and buying opportunities.

## Personal angle
Useful as a buyer-side tool, a hobbyist research engine, and a way to learn a specific collector vehicle market deeply.

## For whom
- enthusiast buyers
- collectors
- dealers
- flippers
- market watchers in a specific vintage niche

## Core problem
Classic vehicle pricing is fragmented across listings, auctions, dealer inventory, and enthusiast channels. It is hard to know what a vehicle is actually worth, what comparable cars traded for, and whether a listing is interesting or just noisy.

## Sharp promise
Understand the real market for a specific vintage vehicle niche and spot compelling listings faster.

## MVP shape
- scrape a few selected sources for one narrow vehicle niche
- normalize listings and comparable sales
- store structured vehicle attributes
- compare asks versus recent comps
- flag potentially interesting listings

## Personal use first
Use it to track markets you personally enjoy and to become more informed before ever buying a car.

## Key validation questions
- Which vehicle niche is the best first wedge?
- Which sources are accessible and reliable enough for useful monitoring?
- Can the system produce actionable comps rather than just a pile of listings?

## Risks
- source fragility / scraping challenges
- messy normalization across listings
- condition and modifications are hard to standardize
- ask prices are not the same as transaction prices

## Next step
Choose the first vehicle niche and identify 2-4 realistic data sources.
